Below is a table listing the top 30 items belonging to Qualified Business Income (QBI) deductions when filing a 2024 tax return with a W-2, formatted as requested with columns for Number, Name, Description, Keywords, Requirements, Deadline, Forms, and Legal Support. The QBI deduction, introduced under the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA) via IRC § 199A, allows eligible taxpayers to deduct up to 20% of their qualified business income from pass-through entities (e.g., sole proprietorships, partnerships, S corporations). For a W-2 holder filing in 2025 (as of March 11, 2025), this table assumes the individual has supplemental business income alongside W-2 wages, as W-2 income itself is not eligible for the QBI deduction.
The QBI deduction applies only to business income, not employee wages, so this list focuses on components, limitations, and related adjustments for a W-2 holder with a side business (e.g., gig work, rental income treated as a trade or business). Since "30 items" exceeds the core QBI elements, I’ve included specific income types, limitations, phase-outs, and related deductions that feed into the QBI calculation, ensuring relevance to a W-2 filer with mixed income. Amounts (e.g., income thresholds) are based on 2023 values ($182,100 single/$364,200 joint) adjusted for 2024 inflation per IRS trends ($191,950 single/$383,900 joint, with phase-out limits at $241,950/$483,900).

Notes:
  1. QBI Deduction Overview: Allows up to 20% deduction on QBI from pass-through entities, plus REIT/PTP income, limited by taxable income minus net capital gains. W-2 wages (employee income) are excluded (IRC § 199A(c)(4)).
  2. W-2 Holder Context: Assumes supplemental business income (e.g., Sch. C gig work, Sch. E rentals as a trade or business) alongside W-2 wages. Pure W-2 income doesn’t qualify.
  3. Forms:
    • Form 8995: Simplified computation for income ≤ $191,950 (single)/$383,900 (joint) est.
    • Form 8995-A: Required for income above thresholds, SSTBs, or co-op income, with schedules (A, C, D).
    • Supporting forms (Sch. C, E, SE, 4562, 8829) feed into QBI calculation.
  4. Deadline: April 15, 2025, standard filing deadline, extendable to October 15 with Form 4868. Quarterly estimated taxes (Form 1040-ES) may apply for business income by January 15, April 15, June 15, and September 15.
  5. Requirements: Must operate a qualified trade or business (not SSTB above thresholds), report net income, and meet income/W-2/UBIA limits if applicable. Documentation (e.g., K-1s, receipts) is critical.
  6. Legal Support: IRC § 199A and Treasury Regulations (e.g., Reg. § 1.199A-1) govern QBI. IRS Publication 535 and Form 8995/8995-A instructions provide guidance.
