Itemized deductions are specific expenses that taxpayers can deduct from Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) to reduce taxable income, claimed on Schedule A (Form 1040) instead of the standard deduction. For a W-2 holder filing in 2025 (as of March 10, 2025) for the 2024 tax year, this table focuses on deductions available under U.S. tax law per the Internal Revenue Code (IRC), tailored to employees with potential supplemental income or personal expenses.
Post-TCJA (Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017), many itemized deductions (e.g., unreimbursed employee expenses) were suspended through 2025, so this list prioritizes those still active for W-2 holders, such as medical expenses, taxes, mortgage interest, and charitable contributions. I’ve included common deductions, special cases, and some business-related items if applicable alongside W-2 income (e.g., Sch. C overlap). Where 2024 specifics (e.g., mileage rates) are estimates, they’re based on 2023 values adjusted per IRS trends.

Notes:
  1. Itemized Deductions Definition: Expenses deducted from AGI on Schedule A, reducing taxable income if total exceeds the standard deduction (e.g., $14,600 single, 2024 est.). Governed by IRC §§ 161-213.
  2. W-2 Focus: Prioritizes deductions for W-2 holders, such as medical (#1, #17-#26), taxes (#2-#4), mortgage interest (#5-#9), and charity (#10-#13, #27-#28). Business-related (#30) applies if supplemental income exists alongside W-2.
  3. Forms:
    • Schedule A: Main form for itemizing, flows to Form 1040, Line 12.
    • Specific forms (e.g., 1098 for mortgage, 8283 for non-cash donations, 4684 for casualty) support calculations.
  4. Deadline: April 15, 2025, is the standard filing deadline, extendable to October 15 with Form 4868. No separate deadline for itemizing unless amending (3-year limit).
  5. Requirements: Must exceed standard deduction to benefit; documentation (e.g., receipts, 1098, doctor letters) is critical. Limits apply (e.g., 7.5% AGI for medical, $10,000 SALT cap).
  6. Legal Support: IRC sections define each deduction. IRS Publication 17 (general), 502 (medical), 526 (charity), and 547 (casualty) provide guidance.
