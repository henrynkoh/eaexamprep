  1. Write down the visions and revelations he received:John was instructed multiple times to record what he saw and heard in his visions (Rev 1:11, 1:19, 14:13, 19:9, 21:5).
  2. Send messages to the seven churches:John was told to write letters to seven specific churches in Asia Minor, conveying Christ's words of encouragement, warning, and exhortation (Rev 1:11, chapters 2-3).
  3. Eat the scroll and prophesy:An angel gave John a scroll to eat, symbolizing his commission to prophesy to many peoples, nations, languages and kings (Rev 10:8-11).
  4. Measure the temple of God:John was given a measuring rod and told to measure the temple of God, the altar, and count the worshippers there (Rev 11:1-2).
  5. Witness and record key apocalyptic visions:Throughout Revelation, John was shown visions of:
  • The throne room of heaven (chapters 4-5)
  • The opening of the seven seals (chapters 6-8)
  • The sounding of the seven trumpets (chapters 8-11)
  • Various symbolic figures and beasts (chapters 12-13)
  • The pouring out of the seven bowls of God's wrath (chapters 15-16)
  • The fall of Babylon (chapters 17-18)
  • The return of Christ and final judgment (chapters 19-20)
  • The new heaven and new earth (chapters 21-22)
  1. Testify to the words of prophecy:John was to bear witness to "the word of God and to the testimony of Jesus Christ" (Rev 1:2) and was told not to seal up the words of prophecy in the book (Rev 22:10).
  2. Warn against altering the prophecy:John was to warn readers not to add to or take away from the words of the prophecy (Rev 22:18-19).
  3. Call people to faithfulness and endurance:Throughout Revelation, John conveys exhortations for believers to remain faithful, overcome, and endure persecution.
