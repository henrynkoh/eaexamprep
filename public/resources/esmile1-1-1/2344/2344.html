The standard deduction is a fixed amount that reduces Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) to determine taxable income, available to taxpayers who do not itemize deductions (e.g., via Schedule A). For a W-2 holder filing in 2025 (as of March 10, 2025) for the 2024 tax year, this table focuses on the standard deduction and its variations, adjustments, or special cases under U.S. tax law per the Internal Revenue Code (IRC).
The standard deduction itself is a single amount based on filing status, with additional amounts for age, blindness, or specific situations (e.g., disaster losses). Where 2024 amounts are estimates (e.g., $14,600 for single filers), they’re based on 2023 values ($13,850) adjusted for inflation per IRS trends.

  2. W-2 Focus: Centers on W-2 holders’ standard deduction (#1-#4), with adjustments for age/blind (#5-#7), dependents (#8, #18, #19), and special cases (#9, #11-#17). Rare scenarios (#20, #21) apply if W-2 holder manages estate/trust.
  3. Forms:

    • Worksheet in 1040 instructions adjusts for dependents (#10) or other cases.
  4. Deadline: April 15, 2025, is the standard filing deadline, extendable to October 15 with Form 4868. No separate deadline for standard deduction unless amending (3-year limit).
  5. Requirements: Based on filing status, age, blindness, dependency, or special events (e.g., disaster). Documentation (e.g., birth date, blindness certification) needed for audits.
  6. Legal Support: IRC § 63 defines the standard deduction and its adjustments. IRS Publication 501 (Dependents, Standard Deduction, and Filing Information) provides guidance.
