These "other taxes" are additional taxes beyond the regular income tax calculated on Form 1040, which may apply to a W-2 holder and are reported on Form 1040, Schedule 2 ("Additional Taxes"). They often arise from specific income sources, activities, or tax situations not covered by standard W-2 wage taxation (e.g., self-employment income, penalties, or special levies). This table is tailored to a W-2 holder filing in 2025 (as of March 10, 2025) for the 2024 tax year, based on current U.S. tax law per the Internal Revenue Code (IRC).
For a W-2 holder, "other taxes" typically supplement the federal income tax withheld from wages (reported on Form W-2, Box 2). I’ve prioritized items relevant to employees, including taxes from supplemental income (e.g., self-employment alongside W-2) or specific tax events (e.g., early retirement distributions). Some taxes are rare for typical W-2 holders but are included for completeness if applicable in mixed-income scenarios.
