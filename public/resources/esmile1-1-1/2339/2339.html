These items represent income sources that are excluded from gross income under U.S. tax law, meaning they do not contribute to taxable income despite being received. For a W-2 holder filing in 2025 (as of March 10, 2025), this table focuses on common exclusions relevant to employees, supplemented by broadly applicable non-taxable items. Gross income, per IRC § 61, includes all income unless specifically excluded by law—these are the exclusions.
Non-taxable income doesn’t typically require reporting on your tax return unless it affects other calculations (e.g., Social Security benefits or credits), but documentation is key for audit purposes. I’ve prioritized items a W-2 holder might encounter, avoiding overly niche or obsolete exclusions, and provided practical details based on current IRS guidelines for the 2024 tax year.
